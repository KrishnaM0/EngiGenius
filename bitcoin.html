Understanding Cryptocurrency: What is Bitcoin?

By Krishna Muskawad

Introduction

Every country has their own currency, which they use as a medium of exchange for goods and services. And these currencies can be in many forms including paper money, coins etc. Which are issued by government or central banks. Whereas,

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual form of currency. Which uses cryptography technology for enhancing security. In today's world, there are many cryptocurrencies available, out of them, Bitcoin is widely used currency.

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a digital currency. It is decentralised and operates on peer-to-peer network without any central authority. It allows users to send and receive money over the internet. Bitcoin was created by an anonymous person or group of people known as Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009. It is not like traditional currencies, which are issued by government and central banks.

How Does Bitcoin Work?

• Blockchain Technology

Blockchain is a public account book, which contains the records of all transactions in a secure and transparent way. The network of computers are used for maintaining this account book. Where these computers called nodes that works together to verify and record transactions.

• Mining

Mining process ensures the security and integrity of the Bitcoin network. Where, this process is used for verifying Bitcoin transactions. The miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical problems. When a problem is solved, a new block of transactions added to the blockchain.

• Wallets

Digital wallets are fundamental for utilizing Bitcoin. Bitcoin wallet is a computer program that permits clients to store and get Bitcoin where, each wallet has a one of a kind address containing long string of characters.

Advantages of Bitcoin

• Security

Cryptographic innovation is utilized for making Bitcoin exchanges secure and irreversible. Where, it is utilized for securing basic data. And there are two fundamental sorts of cryptography and they are: -

1. Symmetric key encryption: - It uses only one key for encryption and decryption of information. This means, the key used for encryption, only that key is needed for decryption.

2. Asymmetric key encryption: - It uses two keys, private key and public key. Where public key is used for encrypting the information and private key is used for decrypting that encrypted information.

• Transparency

Public account book is used for recording all Bitcoin transactions. Which ultimately makes system transparent and trustworthy. And, Bitcoin is operated without any central authorization, making it resistant to government control.

• Low Transaction Fees

Bitcoin transactions contain lower fees compared to traditional banking systems and money transfer services.

• Global Accessibility

Bitcoin can be used by anyone having internet connection. Making it accessible to users around the world.

Challenges of Bitcoin

• Adoption

Bitcoin is growing and gaining popularity. Still, it is not widely accepted yet, as a form of payment. Which is a challenge for enhancing Bitcoins usability and reliability to expand their userbase.

• Volatility

Bitcoins value can be changed suddenly and unexpectedly, which makes it highly volatile and risky to someone for investment.

• Regulation

Governments around the world are still finding the ways to regulate Bitcoin. Which can ultimately lead to legal uncertainties.

• Scalability

When the demand for bitcoin increases, the Bitcoin network can become slow and expensive.

Conclusion

Bitcoin is a digital currency that has potential to revolutionize the way we think about money and transactions. By understanding the basics of how Bitcoin works and its advantages & challenges, we can make better financial decisions on investments. Staying informed and educated is key, whether you are considering Investing in Bitcoin or simply want to learn more about this fascinating technology. Bitcoin represents just-the-beginning of the cryptocurrency era and its influence will grow eventually.
